# Caregiver Resume Screening Guide
## FirstLight Home Care of South Jordan

---

# Purpose

When reviewing caregiver resumes, focus on three primary areas:

1. Relevant credentials
2. Hands-on caregiving experience
3. Reliability and independence

The goal is to identify candidates who can successfully provide one-on-one care in a client's home with minimal supervision while maintaining professionalism, compassion, and reliability.

# Reliability Indicators

Because caregiver reliability directly impacts clients and schedules, look for:

## Positive Indicators

- Multiple years with the same employer
- Consistent employment history
- Reliable transportation mentioned
- Flexible availability
- Weekend availability
- Open scheduling

## Warning Signs

- Frequent job changes
- Several jobs under six months
- Unexplained employment gaps
- No transportation mentioned
- Generic job descriptions
- Limited direct client interaction

---

# Interview Topics to Validate

Even strong resumes should be validated during interviews.

## Attendance and Reliability

- Tell me about your attendance history.
- Have you ever had issues getting to work on time?
- What transportation do you use?

## Independence

- Are you comfortable working alone in a client's home?
- How do you handle situations when a client needs something unexpected?

## Scheduling

- What is your availability?
- How flexible are you with schedule changes?
- Are you willing to work weekends?

## Experience

- Tell me about your experience with dementia clients.
- Describe a challenging client situation and how you handled it.
- What types of transfers are you comfortable performing?

# FirstLight Home Care Ideal Candidate Profile

The ideal FirstLight caregiver:

- Genuinely enjoys helping others
- Is dependable and punctual
- Has reliable transportation
- Communicates professionally
- Works well independently
- Provides excellent companionship
- Maintains client dignity and respect
- Is comfortable with personal care tasks
- Is flexible when client needs change
- Treats caregiving as a profession rather than just a job

Technical skills can be taught. Reliability, compassion, and professionalism are much harder to develop and should be heavily weighted during the hiring process.
